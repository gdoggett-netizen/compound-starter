# Start Here — Why This Works

*Read this before Layer 0. It's the map. Five minutes here saves you an hour of feeling lost later.*

Most tutorials drop you straight into steps — type this, click that — without ever telling you what you're building or why. That's like handing someone tab for a song they've never heard and saying "play." This page is the part that usually gets skipped: the picture on the box before you start the puzzle.

---

## What you're actually building

You're not doing a "tech tutorial." You're building a **second brain** — a system that remembers things for you and quietly does work in the background, so you can stop carrying a hundred open tabs in your head.

Think about how you learn anything physical — a song, a sport, a skill. You don't perform the whole thing at full speed on day one. You learn one piece, get it solid, then add the next. Eventually it's muscle memory and you're not even thinking about it.

This system is built the same way. It comes in **layers**, and each layer works completely on its own before you add the next. You're never grinding toward some far-off payoff — every layer leaves you with something real and useful. Stop whenever it's useful enough. There's no "finishing."

---

## The most important idea: the brain has a brain

Here's the concept that makes everything else click — the part that, if no one tells you, makes the whole thing feel like stabbing in the dark.

Imagine a band. They don't re-explain the key, the tempo, and everyone's role before *every single song*. They set it once, and the band just knows — each person has a job and stays in their lane. That shared understanding is what makes them a band instead of four people making noise.

Your vault has the same thing, and it's the difference between "a pile of scripts" and "a system that actually knows you":

- **`CLAUDE.md`** — the band's shared chart. Who you are, what you care about, how you like things done. Every AI that touches your vault reads this *first*, so you never have to re-explain yourself.
- **Canon** — the stuff that never changes. Your permanent rules and identity, like a band that always tunes the same way. It overrides everything else.
- **Personas** — your bandmates. Different AI roles, each with a specific job, so the right kind of help shows up for the right kind of task.

You'll build the first version of this in Layer 0 without much fuss. The point of knowing about it now is so it doesn't feel like magic later — *this* is the part that makes the system **yours** instead of a generic template.

## Three things that kill the stress

1. **It's free to run.** No credit card, no per-use cost to get started — the AI work runs on Cloudflare's included free tier. If a higher-quality upgrade is ever worth it, the curriculum points it out at the moment it actually matters. You're never forced to spend money to keep going.
2. **You can't really break it.** Everything backs up automatically. If something goes sideways, it's recoverable.
3. **Screenshots are a cheat code.** Stuck on any screen — a confusing settings page, an error, a button you can't find? Take a screenshot (`Cmd+Shift+4` on a Mac) and paste it straight into Claude. It can *see* the image and tell you exactly what to do. Get in this habit early; you'll use it constantly.

## How each layer works

Every layer is a **Builder Agent Prompt** — a page you copy and paste into a Claude Code session on your Mac. Claude then interviews you, one question at a time, and builds *your* version based on your answers. There's no form to fill out and no wrong answers — the more honest and specific you are, the more the system is built for you and not some generic robot.

## One more thing: AI has short-term memory

Worth knowing up front, because you'll run into it. When you work with Claude in a session, it has a **context window** — basically its short-term memory for that conversation. Powerful, but limited. When a session runs long and that memory fills up, Claude **compacts** it: it summarizes the earlier part to make room and keeps going. Handy, but fine detail from early on can get fuzzy — like talking so long you forget how the conversation started.

Two takeaways:

- **Start a fresh session for each new task or layer.** Don't run everything in one endless conversation — a clean session has full, sharp memory.
- **This is exactly why the vault exists.** Your vault is your *long-term* memory — permanent, never forgotten. The session is short-term; the vault is what lasts. The whole system is built so the stuff that matters lives somewhere that doesn't disappear when the window fills up.
